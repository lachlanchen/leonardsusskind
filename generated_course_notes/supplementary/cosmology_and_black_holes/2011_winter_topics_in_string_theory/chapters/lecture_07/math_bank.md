## Definitions And Objects
- \(G_N\): Newton’s constant, treated as the gravitational strength and dimensionally as an area.
- \(g_s\): string coupling, described in the lecture as the amplitude or square root of the probability for string rearrangement when strings cross.
- \(\ell_s\): string length scale; the size of the elementary wiggles or random-walk steps of the string.
- \(\ell_p\): Planck length; the gravitational length scale, with \(\ell_p^2=G_N\).
- \(L\): total length of the long excited string.
- \(S_{\rm str}\): entropy of the long string.
- \(m_{\rm seg}\): mass of one elementary segment of string of length \(\ell_s\).
- \(M\): total mass of the macroscopic object under discussion, either the whole long string or the black hole depending on context.
- \(R_s\): Schwarzschild radius of the black hole, used only at scaling level.
- \(M_0,g_{s,0}\): mass and coupling of the target black hole whose entropy is being inferred.
- \(M_\ast,g_{s,\ast}\): mass and coupling at the string/black-hole crossover point.
- \(A\): horizon area for a black hole; later also the boundary area of a spatial region in the holographic argument.
- \(S_{\max}\): maximum entropy compatible with fitting inside a given region.
- \(a(t)\): cosmological scale factor.
- \(H\): Hubble parameter; called the “Hubble constant” in the lecture, but only truly time-independent in the special de Sitter case.
- Adiabat / isentropic line: the curve in parameter space along which entropy stays fixed during slow change of coupling.
- Holographic principle: the claim that the maximum number of degrees of freedom of a region scales like the boundary area in Planck units.
- de Sitter space: exponentially expanding spacetime with constant \(H\).

## Derivation Steps
Natural units and the dimensions of mass
1. Start with the board convention \(c=\hbar=1\).
2. Use the photon energy relation \(E\sim \hbar c/\lambda\).
3. Convert energy to mass with \(m=E/c^2\).
4. Set \(c=\hbar=1\).
5. Conclude that mass has the dimensions of inverse length: \(m\sim 1/\lambda\).

Dimensions of Newton’s constant
1. Use the Newtonian scaling \(a\sim G_N M/R^2\).
2. In \(c=1\) units, acceleration has dimensions \(L/T^2\sim 1/L\).
3. Replace \(M\) by \(1/L\).
4. Match dimensions on both sides.
5. Conclude \([G_N]=L^2\).
6. Identify that area scale with \(\ell_p^2\), so \(G_N=\ell_p^2\).

Relation between string coupling and gravitational scale
1. Model gravity in string theory as exchange of a closed-string mode with two factors of the splitting/joining amplitude.
2. Infer that gravitational strength is proportional to \(g_s^2\).
3. Since \(g_s\) is dimensionless while \(G_N\) has dimensions of area, introduce the only available length scale \(\ell_s\).
4. Write \(G_N\sim g_s^2 \ell_s^2\).
5. Take square roots to get \(\ell_p=g_s\ell_s\).

Entropy and mass of a long string
1. Model the excited string as a random walk on a lattice with step size \(\ell_s\).
2. Count the number of elementary decisions as proportional to the number of steps.
3. Write \(S_{\rm str}\sim L/\ell_s\).
4. Assign each elementary segment mass \(m_{\rm seg}\sim 1/\ell_s\).
5. Multiply number of segments by segment mass to get \(M\sim (L/\ell_s)(1/\ell_s)=L/\ell_s^2\).
6. Solve for \(L\sim M\ell_s^2\).
7. Substitute into the entropy formula to obtain \(S_{\rm str}\sim M\ell_s\).

Black-hole entropy in mass form
1. Start from \(S_{\rm BH}=A/(4G_N)\).
2. Drop the factor \(1/4\) when doing the scaling argument.
3. Use \(R_s\sim M G_N\).
4. Infer \(A\sim R_s^2\sim M^2 G_N^2\).
5. Divide by \(G_N\).
6. Obtain \(S_{\rm BH}\sim M^2 G_N\sim M^2\ell_p^2\).

Adiabatic correspondence curve
1. Consider slowly decreasing the string coupling while holding \(\ell_s\) fixed.
2. Use adiabatic invariance to keep entropy constant.
3. Use the dimensional statement that black-hole entropy depends only on the dimensionless combination \(M\ell_p\).
4. Replace \(\ell_p\) by \(g_s\ell_s\).
5. With fixed \(\ell_s\), conclude entropy is a function of \(M g_s\).
6. Therefore the constant-entropy curve obeys \(M g_s = M_0 g_{s,0}\).

Crossover curve
1. Define the crossover by the condition that the Schwarzschild radius becomes of order the string scale.
2. Write \(R_s\sim \ell_s\).
3. Replace \(R_s\) by \(M G_N\).
4. Replace \(G_N\) by \(g_s^2\ell_s^2\).
5. Obtain \(M g_s^2 \sim 1/\ell_s\).

Entropy matching at the crossover
1. Solve the two equations \(M_\ast g_{s,\ast}=M_0 g_{s,0}\) and \(M_\ast g_{s,\ast}^2\sim 1/\ell_s\).
2. Square the first equation.
3. Divide the squared first equation by the second to eliminate one power of \(g_{s,\ast}\).
4. Obtain \(M_\ast \sim M_0^2 g_{s,0}^2 \ell_s\).
5. Multiply by \(\ell_s\) to get \(M_\ast\ell_s \sim M_0^2 g_{s,0}^2 \ell_s^2\).
6. Replace \(g_{s,0}^2\ell_s^2\) by \(G_{N,0}\).
7. Read \(M_\ast\ell_s\) as the string entropy at the crossover.
8. Conclude \(S\sim M_0^2 G_{N,0}\sim M_0^2\ell_{p,0}^2\).

Maximum entropy without gravity
1. Divide the room into cells of volume \(V_{\rm cell}\).
2. Ask only whether each cell is occupied or empty.
3. Count states as \(\Omega=2^N\) with \(N\sim V/V_{\rm cell}\).
4. Take the logarithm.
5. Get \(S_{\max}\sim V/V_{\rm cell}\).

Holographic bound from shell collapse
1. Consider a region containing entropy \(S\) and mass \(M\).
2. Assume \(M\) is below the mass of a black hole that would fill the region.
3. Add a shell carrying just enough extra mass to reach that black-hole mass.
4. Let the shell collapse through the boundary.
5. The final state is a black hole filling the region, with entropy \(A/(4G_N)\).
6. Use the second law \(S_{\rm initial}\le S_{\rm final}\).
7. Infer \(S\le A/(4G_N)\).
8. Interpret the maximum entropy as area-scaling rather than volume-scaling.

Proper distance and Hubble law
1. Start from \(ds^2=-dt^2+a(t)^2 dx_i dx_i\).
2. Fix two comoving points with constant coordinate separation \(\Delta x\).
3. Read off the proper distance \(d(t)=a(t)\Delta x\).
4. Differentiate at fixed coordinates.
5. Obtain \(v=\dot d=\Delta x\,\dot a\).
6. Rewrite as \(v=(\dot a/a)d\).
7. Define \(H=\dot a/a\).
8. Get Hubble’s law \(v=Hd\).

Constant \(H\) and de Sitter expansion
1. Assume \(H\) is constant in time.
2. Write \(\dot a/a = H\).
3. Rearrange as \(\dot a = Ha\).
4. Solve the differential equation.
5. Obtain \(a(t)=a_0 e^{Ht}\).
6. Note that the metric uses \(a(t)^2\), so the spatial factor scales like \(e^{2Ht}\).
7. Identify the resulting spacetime as de Sitter space.

## Notation Choices
- Use \(G_N\) for Newton’s constant throughout.
- Use \(g_s\) for the string coupling throughout.
- Use \(\ell_p\) for Planck length and \(\ell_s\) for string length.
- Use \(M\) for the total macroscopic mass; do not alternate between \(m\) and \(M\) for the same object.
- Use \(m_{\rm seg}\) for the elementary string-segment mass.
- Use \(L\) only for the total string length.
- Use \(S_{\rm str}\), \(S_{\rm BH}\), and \(S_{\max}\) to separate the three entropy notions.
- Use \(R_s\) for the Schwarzschild radius.
- Use \(M_0,g_{s,0}\) for the target black hole and \(M_\ast,g_{s,\ast}\) for the crossover point.
- Use \(A\) only for area; do not let it collide with the scale factor.
- Use lowercase \(a(t)\) for the cosmological scale factor.
- Use \(H\) for the Hubble parameter, with a note that the lecture often says “Hubble constant.”
- Use overdots for time derivatives: \(\dot a = da/dt\).
- Use \(\sim\) for scaling relations whenever the lecture is explicitly dropping factors of \(2\), \(4\), or \(2\pi\).

## Uncertain Mathematics
- The lecture’s energy-wavelength formula is dimensional, not precision spectroscopy. The final notes should avoid committing to exact \(2\pi\) conventions and should present \(E\sim \hbar c/\lambda\) as a scaling relation.
- The spoken line around \(E=mc^2\) is jokingly garbled. The notes should reconstruct the intended dimensional step cleanly rather than quote the verbal slip.
- The statement that “\(G\) is also the Planck area squared” should not be preserved literally. The mathematically consistent statement is \(G_N=\ell_p^2\).
- The lecture frequently blurs little \(g\) and big \(G\). The chapter must standardize this, because the raw transcript does not.
- The crossover condition \(R_s\sim \ell_s\) is heuristic and parametric, not a sharply derived phase boundary.
- The simultaneous solution for \(M_\ast\) and \(g_{s,\ast}\) is verbally messy; the notes should present only the cleaned scaling algebra and should not pretend exact equality including order-one coefficients.
- The holographic argument proves an entropy upper bound and then identifies the maximum with the black-hole value by saturation. The final notes should preserve that logical structure.
- In the cosmology section, the transcript slips between \(a\) and \(A\), and also between \(a\) and \(a^2\). The clean note form should use \(a(t)\) for the scale factor and \(a(t)^2\) only inside the metric.
- The line “\(dA/dt = h a\)” in the transcript should be normalized to \(\dot a = Ha\).
- The Hubble-law formula in the lecture is an equal-time statement for comoving points, not directly the same as what one observes from look-back time data; this should be stated cautiously in the chapter.
