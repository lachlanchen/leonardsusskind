Design clean, text-free, full-bleed front-cover artwork for a serious lecture-note volume on cosmology from Leonard Susskind's Theoretical Minimum course.

This must be flat cover art only, not a photographed or rendered physical book, not a mockup, not a poster, and not a YouTube thumbnail.

Visual direction:
- Japanese editorial minimalism with a more vivid but still disciplined scientific mood
- one clear cosmology idea only: cosmic expansion, curved spacetime, horizon geometry, structure growth, or an elegant universe-scale flow
- crisp and deliberate linework with strong line hierarchy
- beautiful and luminous without becoming noisy or ornamental
- clean large forms, clean curves, readable negative space, no messy line tangles
- reserve broad quiet negative space in the upper portion for later LaTeX title overlay

Palette:
- warm cream, deep midnight blue, dark teal, soft charcoal, and one restrained gold or coral accent

Composition:
- portrait 5:7 for a full printed page
- edge-to-edge cover art
- one coherent central image, not a collage
- balanced geometry, atmosphere, and silence

Absolutely avoid:
- any text, letters, numbers, logos, or pseudo-typography
- dense star fields, fake equations, messy graph lines, or cluttered symbolic overlays
- stock-photo clichés, lecture screenshots, watermarks, or 3D product shadows
